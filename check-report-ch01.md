### claim-01-2 — Grade B — PASS (no downgrade)

Claim: 节食触发求生模式：基础代谢下降、饥饿激素急剧上升、大脑对食物敏感度增加；体重调定点保护机制。

Source verification:
- Book text confirmed in `你的最后一本瘦身书.md` 第一章，原话逐字核实。
- PMC5357888 (ghrelin/leptin review): confirmed ghrelin rises significantly with caloric restriction; leptin falls disproportionately to fat loss. **Caveat**: the review's central finding is that ghrelin and leptin changes *alone* do not reliably predict weight regain in free-living humans. The claim as framed in the chapter (mechanism exists, not predictive precision) is not invalidated, but PMC5357888 is a weaker citation than presented — it establishes the hormonal direction, not the mechanistic inevitability.
- NBK592402 (Set-Point Theory, StatPearls): confirmed neurohormonal changes, adaptive thermogenesis (10% weight loss → 20–25% reduction in total energy expenditure), and that "over 80% of individuals eventually regain the weight." This is a stronger citation for the set-point framing.

Grade B stands: the mechanism is well-documented in two live external sources. The chapter prose already hedges appropriately ("外部研究证实了这一机制"). B confirmed.

## Adversarial Pass

### Biggest Overstatement in Chapter Prose

**The set-point/反弹 framing is presented as more mechanistically deterministic than the science warrants.**

The chapter states: "节食会让你的身体*更想吃*，同时*更难消耗*" and "体重调定点保护机制——你越用力对抗，身体越用力守住原来的重量." This is presented near-deterministically. The primary citation (PMC5357888) explicitly concludes that ghrelin and leptin changes *alone* are insufficient to predict weight regain in free-living humans. Real-world outcomes are heterogeneous: some individuals do sustain long-term weight loss (estimates range 10–33% depending on definition). The chapter's hedges ("外部研究证实了这一机制") and the card's B-grade are appropriate, but the prose "越用力对抗，身体越用力守住" risks reading as absolute biological law rather than a strong statistical tendency with meaningful individual variation.

**Recommended fix**: add one clause such as "对大多数人而言" or "在统计意义上" to the set-point sentence to prevent a deterministic reading.

### Strongest Counterargument a Skeptical Reader Would Raise

**"If obesity is never anyone's 'choice,' where does personal agency go?"**

The chapter's core thesis — that no one ever chooses obesity — is logically in tension with the book's own premise (that reading this book can produce change). The raw source itself acknowledges this contradiction: "但与此同时，肥胖又是所有肥胖者欲望的结果。毕竟没有人逼你吃下那一口" (你的最后一本瘦身书). A skeptical reader will note that stripping all agency from the "falling in" phase while simultaneously claiming the reader *can* escape undermines coherence. The chapter does not address this tension. The drafter should add a bridging line in ch01 or ch02 that distinguishes "you didn't choose the trap" from "you can choose to walk out" — otherwise the framing risks being read as either fatalistic or logically inconsistent.
